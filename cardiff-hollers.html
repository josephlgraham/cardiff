Hills & Hollers

The classified section for Cardiff and the Five Mile Creek watershed. Free to post, free to read. No account required. Think of it as the community bulletin board that used to hang at the feed store — for everyone in the watershed.

Featured Holler
A page that can carry both a fence-post laugh and a real local need.

Hills & Hollers is where the site loosens its collar a little. It can hold a porch joke, a chainsaw recommendation, a memory about the old road, a note about something odd on the creek, or a neighbor asking who still knows how to fix a thing right.

That mix matters. A community page should feel useful, but it should also sound like people actually live here.

Use it for neighbor-to-neighbor texture, not for official notice. This is the porch, not the podium.

What you will find here

🤝
Seeking & Offering Help
Need a hand moving something heavy? Got tools to lend? Know someone who does good work? This is the place. Cardiff takes care of Cardiff.
Examples: firewood split, fence repair, chainsaw work, rides to town
🌿
Free & For Sale
Extra vegetables from the garden. Seeds to share. Something in the barn you are not using. Barter welcome. No big business listings — this is neighbors trading with neighbors.
Examples: tomato starts, canned goods, equipment, animals
📅
Community Events
Workdays, cleanups, meetings, gatherings. If it involves Cardiff people and Cardiff ground, it belongs here. Watershed cleanup days are especially welcome.
Examples: creek cleanup, council meetings, cookouts, workdays
🔍
Looking For
Searching for a specific person, a piece of Cardiff history, an old photograph, or someone who remembers? Post it here. This community has a long memory.
Examples: family history, old photos, property records, stories
🌊
Watershed Watch
Spotted illegal dumping? Unusual activity on the creek? Wildlife worth documenting? Water conditions worth noting? Cardiff is upstream. What happens here matters downstream.
Examples: dumping reports, unusual sightings, water quality observations
📖
Stories & History
Things you remember about Cardiff. Things your grandparents told you. History that is not written down anywhere. We want to write it down while we still can.
Examples: flood memories, family stories, old Cardiff, the way things were

The spirit of the thing: The old Farmer's Almanac ran classified ads between the frost date tables and the moon calendars. Reader's Digest ran "Life in These United States" — short true stories from real people. Hills & Hollers is that tradition for Cardiff and the Five Mile Creek watershed. No algorithms. No advertising. Just neighbors talking to neighbors.
