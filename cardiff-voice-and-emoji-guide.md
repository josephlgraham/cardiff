# Cardiff Voice and Emoji Guide

## North Star

This site should feel like Cardiff has its own public voice:

- Kind
- Calm
- Deeply informed
- Never showy about its knowledge
- Neighborly without sounding soft-headed

The best reference point is **Outdoor Alabama with Doug Phillips**:

- warm and caring
- observant
- trustworthy
- quietly wise
- rich in real field knowledge
- never trying too hard

If a page sounds smug, too online, too brand-strategy polished, or too cute to trust, pull it back.

## Core Voice

- Write like someone who knows the land, the creek, the weather, and the town.
- Explain things clearly enough for a neighbor, but never flatten the intelligence out of them.
- Use plain language first, then layer in depth.
- Let the writing feel local and lived-in.
- Sound encouraging, not salesy.
- Be gentle with people and firm with facts.

## Tone Rules

- Friendly, never flippant.
- Serious when the subject is civic, storm-related, or safety-related.
- Playful in small touches, not in the backbone of the copy.
- Curious and grounded instead of dramatic.
- A little poetic is fine if it still reads clearly on a town website.

## Emoji Layer

Emojis are important here. They should make the site feel human, warm, and memorable.

### Use emojis for

- pills
- infobox labels
- section cues
- weather and nature states
- skywatch and seasonal markers
- reaction widgets

### Emoji principles

- Use emojis as seasoning, not the whole meal.
- One strong emoji is usually better than three.
- Pick emojis that improve scanning or add warmth.
- Match the emoji to the real subject matter.
- Keep the tone charming, not goofy.

### Good recurring emoji families

- Weather: `🌤` `🌧` `🌬` `☀️` `⛈️`
- Creek and water: `🌊` `💧` `🐟` `🎣`
- Sky: `🌙` `☄️` `🔭` `✨`
- Seasons and wildlife: `🌱` `🍃` `🫐` `🦉` `🐦` `🦌` `🦋`
- Civic life: `🧭` `🏛️` `📝` `🤝` `🚧`
- Community energy: `❤️` `🌿` `👣`

### Avoid

- random meme energy
- sarcastic emoji use
- emoji spam
- using emojis where the subject is grief, legal risk, or direct danger

## Language Patterns We Want

- "Right now around Cardiff..."
- "What this means on the ground..."
- "A good month for..."
- "Worth watching..."
- "The town does not need mystery here."
- "A healthy town starts with..."
- "This is a small thing, but it matters."

## Language Patterns To Avoid

- startup language
- tourism brochure language
- generic AI phrases like "delve," "vibrant," or "rich tapestry"
- fake folksiness
- smug jokes
- big heroic language about "transforming" everything

## Page-by-Page Voice

### Almanac

- Feels like a seasoned local naturalist with good porch manners.
- Can be lyrical in short bursts.
- Should mix observation, field sense, and science.
- Emojis should be lively here.

### Civic

- Clear, steady, plainspoken.
- More restraint.
- Emojis are welcome, but they should help navigation and approachability more than whimsy.
- Never make civic dysfunction sound cute.

### Calendar

- Practical first.
- Still local in tone.
- Date language should feel useful, not bureaucratic.

### Field Guide / Cemetery / Get Involved

- Human, affectionate, rooted.
- Strong sense that these places matter because people matter.

## Writing Test

Before shipping copy, ask:

1. Does this sound like it cares about Cardiff?
2. Does it sound like it knows what it is talking about?
3. Would a neighbor trust this voice?
4. Are the emojis helping the page feel alive without making it silly?

If the answer is not clearly yes, rewrite.

## Design Companion Notes

- Plus Jakarta Sans and DM Mono stay the core type pairing.
- Cardiff red stays the anchor color.
- No dark mode by default.
- Rounded corners and small visual warmth are part of the tone.
- Infoboxes should only appear when they are actually useful.
- If a card fits better on a different page, move it there.

## Final Standard

The site should feel like **a generous local guide with real knowledge**.

Not a brand voice.
Not a government pamphlet.
Not a joke machine.

Kind. Wise. Clear. Local. A little fun. Worth trusting.
